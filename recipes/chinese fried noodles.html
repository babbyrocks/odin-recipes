Chinese Fried Noodles

Ingredients

Noodles and Vegetables

  • 8 oz Chinese egg noodles (or spaghetti), cooked and drained
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1/2 cup julienned carrots
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup green onions, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or sesame oil

Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dark soy sauce (for color, optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper or black pepper

Steps

  1. Prepare the sauce: In a small bowl, mix soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, sugar, and pepper. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add garlic and ginger, and stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add cabbage, carrots, and bell pepper. Stir-fry for 2–3 minutes until vegetables are slightly tender but still crisp.
  4. Add cooked noodles to the pan. Toss well to combine with the vegetables.
  5. Pour in the sauce mixture. Stir-fry everything together for 2–4 minutes until noodles are evenly coated and heated through.
  6. Add bean sprouts and green onions, and stir-fry for 1 more minute.
  7. Serve hot, optionally garnished with extra green onions or sesame seeds.

Cook's Note

Use cold, cooked noodles to avoid them turning mushy. You can cook them ahead and refrigerate for at least 1 hour before stir-frying.
